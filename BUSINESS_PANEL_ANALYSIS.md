## Executive Summary

Everyday Food is a recipe management and meal planning app competing in a $6.4B global recipe app market growing at 10.5% CAGR. The app has ~190+ feature points across recipe management, meal planning, shopping lists, cooking mode, social features, gamification, and a subscription system.

**Key Finding:** Everyday Food sits uniquely between Recime (recipe organization) and Cal AI (calorie tracking). You don't need to copy either. Your strongest play is the **all-in-one cooking companion** that neither competitor offers — combining recipe management, meal planning, shopping, cooking mode, social features, AND gamification in a single app.

## Your Competitive Advantages

### 1. Cook Mode (UNIQUE - Neither Competitor Has This)
Step-by-step cooking with voice guidance, timers, screen-awake, and completion celebration. This is your **killer feature**. Recime helps you FIND recipes. Cal AI helps you COUNT calories. Everyday Food helps you actually COOK.

### 2. Gamification System (UNIQUE - Neither Competitor Has This)
XP, levels, achievements, challenges, leaderboards, streaks. This is a powerful retention mechanism that neither competitor offers. Duolingo proved gamification drives daily engagement.

### 3. Social Features (UNIQUE at this depth)
Full friend system with requests, blocking, recipe sharing, leaderboards. Recime has no social. Cal AI has no social. You have a social cooking network.

### 4. All-in-One Experience
You're the only app that covers the FULL cooking journey:
- **Discover** recipes (search, browse, import)
- **Plan** meals (calendar, meal types)
- **Shop** for ingredients (auto-lists, aisle sorting)
- **Cook** step-by-step (cook mode, voice, timers)
- **Track** nutrition (calories, macros)
- **Share** with friends (social, sharing)
- **Progress** through gamification (XP, achievements)

### 5. Neo-Brutalist Design Identity
Distinctive visual identity that stands out in a market full of generic white-and-green food apps.

## Expert Panel Analysis

### Clayton Christensen — Jobs-to-be-Done & Disruption Theory

> **"Don't ask what features competitors have. Ask what job the customer is hiring the app to do."**

**Analysis:** There are 4 distinct jobs-to-be-done in the cooking space:

| Job | Recime | Cal AI | Everyday Food |
|-----|--------|--------|---------------|
| "Help me find & save recipes I discover online" | Primary job | No | Partial |
| "Help me count calories effortlessly" | No | Primary job | No |
| "Help me plan, shop, and cook meals every day" | Partial | No | **Primary job** |
| "Make cooking fun and keep me motivated" | No | No | **Unique job** |

**Recommendation:** Own jobs #3 and #4 completely. Don't try to out-import Recime or out-track Cal AI. Your moat is the daily cooking companion that makes the entire cooking journey seamless and fun. The gamification system addresses a job nobody else even recognizes exists — making home cooking a habit, not a chore.

**Disruption Angle:** You can disrupt from below by being "good enough" at recipe import while being 10x better at the cooking experience itself. Most recipe apps stop at organizing — you continue through to the actual cooking.

### Michael Porter — Competitive Strategy & Five Forces

> **"The essence of strategy is choosing what NOT to do."**

**Five Forces Analysis:**

| Force | Threat Level | Notes |
|-------|-------------|-------|
| **New Entrants** | HIGH | Low barrier, many indie developers |
| **Substitutes** | HIGH | Physical cookbooks, YouTube, food blogs, ChatGPT |
| **Buyer Power** | HIGH | Zero switching cost, free alternatives everywhere |
| **Supplier Power** | LOW | Recipe data is abundant and free |
| **Rivalry** | VERY HIGH | Recime (10M users), Cal AI (5M users), Mealime, Paprika, Plan to Eat |

**Strategic Position — Differentiation, Not Cost:**

Do NOT compete on price. At EUR 8/month you're already higher than both competitors. You must justify premium pricing through differentiated value.

**What NOT to do:**
- Do NOT build a full calorie counter with photo AI. Cal AI has $1.4M/month revenue and deep AI investment. You will lose.
- Do NOT try to be the best recipe importer. Recime has 10M users and imports from 6+ social platforms. You will be second-best.

**What TO do:**
- Own the "cooking experience" niche. Nobody does cook mode + gamification + social.
- Build switching costs through: recipe collections, cooking history, achievement progress, friend networks, streak data.
- Network effects: friend features create natural virality and switching costs.

### Pricing Assessment

**Current pricing (EUR 8/month) is TOO HIGH for the current market position.** Here's why:

- Recime at $5/month has 10M users and more features in recipe import
- Most meal planning apps cluster around $4-6/month
- You have no annual discount option (leaving money on the table)
- No monthly plan creates friction — users can't "try" at low commitment

### Recommended Pricing Structure

| Tier | Price | Rationale |
|------|-------|-----------|
| **Free** | $0 | 10 recipes, 3-day meal planning, 1 shopping list, basic cook mode |
| **Monthly** | $5.99/month | Competitive with market, low commitment entry |
| **Annual** | $39.99/year (~$3.33/mo) | 44% savings, drives annual commitment |
| **Founding Member** (launch promo) | $29.99/year first year | Early adopter lock-in, build initial user base |

**Why lower than EUR 8/month:**
- You're a NEW app competing against established players with millions of users
- Price is the #1 barrier to conversion for new apps
- Your gamification and social features create natural retention — you don't need price to retain
- Lower price = more users = more network effects = more value per user
- Once you have 100K+ users and proven engagement, you can increase pricing

**Why NOT free:**
- Free devalues the product and attracts low-quality users
- The freemium tier gives enough to demonstrate value
- Premium should feel like an upgrade, not a paywall

## Risk Assessment

### Threats

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| Recime adds cook mode | Medium | High | Move fast, build deep — they're focused on import |
| Cal AI adds meal planning | Low | Medium | They're focused on calorie tracking AI |
| New AI-native cooking app | High | High | Gamification + social create switching costs |
| Low user acquisition | High | Critical | Aggressive freemium + viral loops + content marketing |
| High churn rate | Medium | High | Gamification + streaks + social accountability |

### Antifragility Considerations (Nassim Taleb)

- **Don't bet everything on one feature.** Your diversified feature set is actually a strength — if AI photo recognition becomes commoditized, you still have cook mode, gamification, social.
- **Build optionality.** The platform architecture (Convex + React Native) allows rapid experimentation. Ship fast, measure, keep what works.
- **Social features create antifragility.** User-generated content (shared recipes, friend networks) becomes more valuable with time and harder for competitors to replicate.

## Panel Consensus & Disagreements

### Consensus Points (All Experts Agree)

1. **Do NOT try to compete directly with Cal AI on photo-based calorie tracking** — it's their entire product and they have deep AI investment
2. **Cook Mode + Gamification is the competitive moat** — no competitor has this combination
3. **Current pricing (EUR 8/month) needs adjustment** — too high for market entry, no annual option
4. **Social media recipe import is the #1 missing feature** — table stakes in 2026
5. **The "Duolingo for cooking" positioning is powerful** and immediately communicatable

### Disagreement: Feature Breadth vs Depth

- **Porter & Drucker** argue for radical focus: strip everything that isn't cook mode + gamification + social. Be the best at ONE thing.
- **Kim & Mauborgne** argue the all-in-one approach IS the blue ocean — no one else covers the full journey. Keep breadth but raise quality on key differentiators.
- **Godin** sides with the "tribe" approach: it doesn't matter how many features you have if you don't have a passionate community of 1,000 true fans first.

**Resolution:** Keep the all-in-one breadth (it's already built), but MARKET the app around cook mode + gamification. Lead with the unique, sell the complete.
